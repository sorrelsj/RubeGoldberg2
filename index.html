    • How It Works

    My part of the machine needs paired Arduino code for it to function properly. The input from the previous machine will start the process by actuating the gate at the top and allowing the cart to begin rolling down the ramp. This gate is connected to the Arduino circuitry, and it is implementing a short delay for the second gate. After this delay, the gate will open which allows for a small steel ball to drop into the cart. Afterwards, the cart will travel through the rest of the machine with the ball safely tucked inside. At the end of the last ramp, the cart will hit a lip which will eject the steel ball out of the cart and through the exit door at the end of the machine, becoming the input for the next part of the entire machine.

    Reflection

    This project has been a challenging yet rewarding experience, especially as it was my first design project. Initially, I struggled to determine exactly what I wanted to achieve, and in hindsight, I believe I could have developed a more refined and effective design. The process of conceptualizing and planning took much longer than expected, highlighting areas where I can improve my efficiency in future projects.

    One key takeaway is the importance of refining the design of the tracks. I recommend making the tracks wider and including more robust guard rails to ensure smoother operation. Additionally, the Arduino code, which I planned to implement, was never fully tested. I anticipate that prototyping this design would reveal numerous issues with the gates and the timing of the delays. This highlights the need to prioritize early prototyping and testing to identify and resolve potential challenges earlier in the process.

    Overall, this project has taught me valuable lessons about planning, design iteration, and the importance of testing. These insights will undoubtedly inform my approach to future projects and help me achieve better results.
