Potential Outputs

Potential Output #1: My plan was to have a small hammer (or something similar) come down and strike something. This could be the input to the next machine, or it could strike a seesaw, creating an upward force for the next machine's input to react to. The force created by the hammer will be regulated by frictional forces, so the magnitude of the force should not be very high (going for about 40 newtons here).

Potential Output #2: I am not opposed to the final output being a ball & track design. The ball would only weigh about a pound and would be large enough to actuate whatever needs to be actuated for the next machine's input. It would be traveling between 2 - 4 m/s in order to create enough force.

Potential Output #3: I would like to include a waterwheel in my design, so the final output could be an amount of water being poured into something. The input for the next machine would need to be a container of some sort, and the container would have to be large enough to take in enough water to actually actuate the machine. The water would not be flowing very fast, probably 1 liter every 10 seconds - or 0.1 liters/second.
